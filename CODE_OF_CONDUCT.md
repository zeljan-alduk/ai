# Code of Conduct

Meridian is a working project. We want contributors to spend their energy
on the code, not on managing each other's behavior. This document is short
on purpose.

## Principles

1. **Assume good faith.** Most disagreements are about taste or context, not
   intent. Ask before you accuse.
2. **Be direct, not personal.** Critique code, designs, and arguments —
   never the person making them. Reviews are about the work.
3. **Keep it professional.** Same standard you'd want in a pull-request
   review at a serious workplace.
4. **Inclusive by default.** Everyone working in good faith is welcome,
   regardless of background, identity, experience level, employer, or
   choice of editor.
5. **Off-topic stays off-topic.** Project channels are for the project.
6. **Respect maintainer time.** Do your homework before opening an issue.
   Read the docs, search existing issues, write a clear repro.
7. **No spam, no self-promotion, no recruiting.** First offense: warning.
   Second: removal.

## Scope

This applies to all project spaces: issues, pull requests, discussions,
review comments, commit messages, and any chat or call associated with
the project. It also applies to public statements made about the project
in connection with one's contributor identity.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may delete comments, close issues or PRs, lock conversations,
or remove contributors who breach these principles. We will make
proportional decisions and explain them in writing.

If you experience or witness a breach, contact the maintainers privately
at `conduct@meridian-labs.example` (replace once the legal entity is
registered). We will respond within five business days. Reports are
confidential to the people responsible for resolution.

Attempts to retaliate against someone who reports an issue, or to
weaponize this document to silence legitimate technical disagreement,
are themselves breaches.
